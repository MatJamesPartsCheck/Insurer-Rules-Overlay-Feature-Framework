Top Prioritisation Insights — Updated
01
Availability now confirmed by 9 of 12 participants. It is load-bearing for auto-select trust and must ship together — not after. Shari and Liam both independently confirmed stock visibility is a core decision input, not a nice-to-have.
02
Terminology is a silent adoption risk. "In scope / Out of scope" confused 2 users. Pricing % basis (markup vs. of list) confused Shari so much she said she'd "personally hate" the screen. These are NOW fixes — no new development required, just copy.
03
The iBodyshop sync bug is a critical blocker. Notes wiped on every sync event — confirmed, ongoing, screenshots sent. This needs an engineering fix tracked separately from the IRO project, but it will undermine adoption of any note-related features until resolved.
04
Auto-select vs. Cheapest distinction not self-evident. Shari's mental model is "click cheapest, then check stock manually." She directly asked what's different about auto-select. If the UI doesn't answer this without explanation, adoption will stall at the most critical point.
05
Allianz combination rules expose a structural gap. Liam manually evaluates OR/AND pricing logic per job. The rules engine supports a single method only. Until conditional logic is supported, auto-select will make sub-optimal Allianz parallel selections — affecting a key insurer relationship.
