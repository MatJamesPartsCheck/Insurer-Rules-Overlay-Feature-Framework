Top 5 Prioritisation Insights
01
Availability is load-bearing, not a nice-to-have. 7 of 10 participants raised it unprompted. Auto-select will be distrusted or overridden until stock status and lead times are visible on the grid. It must ship alongside auto-select, not after it.
02
List-minus pricing is a silent MSO blocker. Only 1 mention but outsized revenue impact — MSOs are ~80% of PartsCheck revenue. Allianz mandates list-minus on OEM parts. Without it, auto-select ships broken for the highest-value segment.
03
Discoverability is a prerequisite for everything. 3 repairers had never heard of insurer rules. Feature richness is irrelevant if users don't know the feature exists. Pre-built templates and a standalone settings page unlock adoption.
04
"Guide, don't decide" is a design constraint. Experienced estimators factor in supplier relationships, part re-labelling, and fitment risk — context the system cannot know. Auto-select must be a toggle and a starting point, never a default final state.
05
MSO and indie repairer needs diverge sharply. Basket logic, policy-level rules, RBAC, and approval workflows only matter at MSO scale. Use a segment lens alongside RICE when making final calls.
