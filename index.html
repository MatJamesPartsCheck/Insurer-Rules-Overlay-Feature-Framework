Automation / Auto-Select 6 features
Feature Description Mentions Suggested Final Priority
Auto-select is optional and easily overridable
"Guide, don't decide" — auto-select fires as a button/toggle, not a default; experienced estimators retain full manual control over every selection.
5 users
 NOW
Auto-select triggers on job load, not just button click
Repairers want the system to pre-apply selections when they open a job number, removing an extra click and streamlining the quote start.
2 users
 NOW
Basket-of-goods auto-select logic (not line-by-line)
Auto-select evaluates the full basket — supplier response mix, relative pricing, availability trade-offs — rather than optimising each line independently.

Trust & Transparency 4 features
Feature Description Mentions Suggested Final Priority
Compliance behaviour: flag / warn vs. hard block
Repairers strongly prefer "allow but warn / flag non-compliant" over preventing selection; hard blocks remove necessary override capability for legitimate edge cases.
3 users
 NOW
Horizontal summary bar with cost, sell, profit & insurer saving
Top-of-screen totals panel showing full margin breakdown per job — strongly preferred over the side panel layout; validates profitability before committing to selections.
4 users
 NOW
Part number match indicator clearly visible and labelled
Grey exclamation / green shield icons are misunderstood; need clear labelling or tooltip so repairers understand part number match vs. supplier comment status — long-standing misunderstanding is affecting selection behaviour.
2 users
 NOW
Supplier part data integrity — OEM vs. aftermarket misclassification
Some suppliers mislabel aftermarket parts as OEM in their listings; if uncorrected at source, compliance calculations are inaccurate and repairers lose trust in the system.
